Ratan Naval Tata

1937 – 2024 | Visionary, Philanthropist, Legend

"I don't believe in taking right decisions. I take decisions and then make them right."

About the Legend

Ratan Tata was an Indian industrialist and former chairman of Tata Sons. Under his leadership, the Tata Group transformed from a largely India-centric group into a global business empire, while maintaining a deep commitment to ethics and philanthropy.

Key Milestones

  • 1991: Took over as Chairman of the Tata Group, succeeding J.R.D. Tata.
  • 2000: Awarded the Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian honor.
  • 2004: Oversaw the acquisition of Tetley, making Tata Tea a global brand.
  • 2008: Launched the Tata Nano, aiming to provide affordable mobility for the common man.
  • 2008: Awarded the Padma Vibhushan, India's second-highest civilian honor.
  • Philanthropy: Over 60% of Tata's wealth is held by charitable trusts, supporting education, medicine, and rural development.

Legacy

Beyond the multi-billion dollar acquisitions like Jaguar Land Rover and Corus, Ratan Tata is remembered for his humility, his love for animals (especially dogs), and his unwavering moral compass. He proved that business success and social responsibility can go hand-in-hand.
